# DAO Treasury Disclosure Policy

## Status

This is the DAO operating policy for treasury transparency and disclosure boundaries. Public UI surfaces, governance proposals, reporting packets, and treasury dashboards should follow this policy unless the DAO intentionally publishes a narrower or broader disclosure through an approved governance process.

## Public Transparency

The DAO may publish information that is already public on-chain or needed to verify DAO custody and governance execution:

- On-chain treasury, converter, staking, escrow, and payroll contract balances.
- Executed transactions, public proposal records, timelock status, and role-controlled contract addresses.
- Reserve categories and current TUT quantities needed to verify DAO custody and conversion coverage.

Public reporting should use utility, governance, and custody language. It should avoid speculative valuation framing, investment language, or unpublished market/liquidity plans.

## Private By DAO Policy

The following information must stay private unless intentionally published through an approved DAO disclosure:

- Off-chain bank balances or fiat reserves.
- Payroll recipient details.
- Vendor/invoice details before approval.
- Planned treasury moves before governance/public execution.
- Safe signer personal/security details.
- Internal runway, burn rate, or legal/accounting strategy unless intentionally published.

## Operating Rules

- Treasury dashboards may display public on-chain balances and executed governance activity.
- Treasury dashboards must not expose private operating records, counterparty details, or pre-approval treasury strategy.
- Treasury proposals should disclose enough context for governance review while withholding private details until approval or an intentional public disclosure.
- Any transfer from the timelock-owned treasury to the Gnosis Safe should be limited to a defined operating purpose, amount, and review window.
- Safe signer security details must be handled out of band and should not be embedded in public repositories, dashboards, proposal text, or training materials.
