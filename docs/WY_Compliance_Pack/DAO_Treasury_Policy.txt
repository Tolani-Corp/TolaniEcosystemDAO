                    SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

1.1 - Purpose

This Treasury Policy establishes the governance framework, operational 
procedures, and risk management protocols for the management of Tolani 
Ecosystem DAO LLC's financial assets across both traditional (fiat) and 
blockchain-based (on-chain) treasury systems.

1.2 - Scope

This policy applies to:
(a) All on-chain assets held in DAO smart contracts and wallets
(b) Fiat currency held in traditional banking accounts
(c) Digital assets across all blockchain networks
(d) Any financial instruments or investments held by the DAO
(e) All persons authorized to manage treasury assets

1.3 - Objectives

The Treasury shall be managed to:
(a) Preserve capital and maintain liquidity for operations
(b) Support the DAO's educational mission and ecosystem development
(c) Generate sustainable yield while managing risk
(d) Maintain regulatory compliance across jurisdictions
(e) Ensure transparency and accountability to Members

================================================================================
           SECTION 3: SPENDING AUTHORITY AND APPROVAL THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

3.1 - Approval Tiers

TIER 1 - ROUTINE OPERATIONS (Up to $5,000 USD equivalent)
- Approval: Single authorized signer
- Documentation: Expense report within 7 days
- Examples: Software subscriptions, minor services

TIER 2 - OPERATIONAL EXPENSES ($5,001 - $25,000 USD equivalent)
- Approval: 2-of-5 Gnosis Safe signers
- Documentation: Prior approval request + expense report
- Examples: Contractor payments, equipment

TIER 3 - SIGNIFICANT EXPENDITURES ($25,001 - $100,000 USD equivalent)
- Approval: 3-of-5 Gnosis Safe signers + Community notification
- Documentation: Full proposal with budget justification
- Examples: Major development projects, partnerships

TIER 4 - MAJOR EXPENDITURES (Over $100,000 USD equivalent)
- Approval: Full governance vote (quorum + majority)
- Documentation: Governance proposal with 7-day voting
- Examples: Strategic investments, major grants

3.2 - Budget Categories

The DAO maintains annual budgets for the following categories:

| Category                  | Target Allocation | Approval Authority    |
|--------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------|
| Technology & Development | 35%               | Tier 2-4 based on amount |
| Educational Programs     | 25%               | Tier 2-4 based on amount |
| Operations & Admin       | 15%               | Tier 1-3 based on amount |
| Marketing & Growth       | 10%               | Tier 2-3 based on amount |
| Legal & Compliance       | 10%               | Tier 2-4 based on amount |
| Reserves                 | 5%                | Governance only       |

3.3 - Prohibited Expenditures

The following expenditures are prohibited without supermajority (66%) 
governance approval:

(a) Loans to Members, signers, or related parties
(b) Speculative investments exceeding 10% of treasury
(c) Political contributions or lobbying
(d) Personal expenses of any individual
(e) Transactions with sanctioned entities or persons

================================================================================
               SECTION 4: MULTI-SIGNATURE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

4.1 - Gnosis Safe Configuration

PRIMARY MULTI-SIG: 0xa56eb5E3990C740C8c58F02eAD263feF02567677
- Threshold: 3-of-5 signatures required
- Network: Ethereum Mainnet (bridged to Base as needed)

4.2 - Signer Responsibilities

Each authorized signer agrees to:
(a) Secure their signing keys with hardware wallets
(b) Respond to signing requests within 48 hours
(c) Verify transaction details before signing
(d) Report any security concerns immediately
(e) Participate in quarterly security reviews

4.3 - Signer Selection and Removal

NEW SIGNERS:
- Nominated through governance proposal
- Approved by majority vote
- KYC/identity verification required
- Hardware wallet mandatory

SIGNER REMOVAL:
- Governance vote or emergency removal
- Emergency removal: 4-of-5 remaining signers
- Immediate key rotation upon removal

4.4 - Transaction Verification

Before signing any transaction, signers must verify:
(a) Recipient address is correct and expected
(b) Amount matches approved budget/proposal
(c) Transaction purpose is documented
(d) Appropriate approval tier requirements met
(e) No unusual or suspicious patterns

================================================================================
                  SECTION 5: INVESTMENT GUIDELINES
================================================================================

5.1 - Permitted Investments

CONSERVATIVE (Up to 100% of investable assets):
- USDC, USDT, DAI stablecoins
- US Treasury-backed instruments
- Money market funds
- Blue-chip protocol staking (ETH staking, etc.)

MODERATE (Up to 50% of investable assets):
- Ethereum (ETH)
- Established DeFi protocols (Aave, Compound, Uniswap)
- Protocol-owned liquidity positions
- Revenue-generating protocol positions

GROWTH (Up to 20% of investable assets):
- Strategic ecosystem investments
- Early-stage protocol positions
- Venture investments (governance approved)

5.2 - Prohibited Investments

(a) Unaudited or newly launched protocols
(b) Leveraged positions exceeding 2x
(c) Derivative instruments without hedging purpose
(d) Anonymous or privacy tokens in significant amounts
(e) Securities without proper legal analysis
(f) Investments in sanctioned jurisdictions

5.3 - Yield Optimization

Yield generation strategies must:
(a) Use only audited protocols
(b) Maintain adequate liquidity for redemption
(c) Consider smart contract risk in position sizing
(d) Be reviewed quarterly for continued appropriateness
(e) Be disclosed to Members through treasury reports

================================================================================
                  SECTION 6: FIAT BANKING OPERATIONS
================================================================================

6.1 - Bank Account Management

The DAO maintains fiat banking relationships for:
(a) Receiving donations and grants
(b) Paying vendors and contractors
(c) Payroll (if applicable)
(d) Regulatory compliance requirements
(e) Off-ramp for digital asset liquidation

6.2 - Authorized Signers (Fiat)

Bank accounts require dual authorization for:
(a) Wire transfers over $10,000
(b) ACH payments over $25,000
(c) Account changes or new accounts
(d) Investment transactions

6.3 - Fiat-Crypto Bridge

Converting between fiat and cryptocurrency:
(a) Use only licensed and compliant exchanges
(b) Document all conversions with timestamps
(c) Maintain audit trail for tax purposes
(d) Conversions over $50,000 require Tier 3 approval

6.4 - Banking Compliance

The DAO shall:
(a) Maintain complete transaction records
(b) File required tax documents (1099s, etc.)
(c) Comply with AML/KYC requirements
(d) Respond to regulatory inquiries promptly
(e) Maintain appropriate insurance coverage

================================================================================
               SECTION 7: ON-CHAIN TREASURY OPERATIONS
================================================================================

7.1 - Transaction Standards

All on-chain transactions shall:
(a) Use established, audited contracts
(b) Include clear documentation and purpose
(c) Be executed through multi-sig or governance
(d) Be recorded in treasury logs
(e) Be visible on public block explorers

7.2 - Gas Management

The treasury shall:
(a) Maintain ETH reserves for gas costs
(b) Use gas optimization strategies
(c) Batch transactions when practical
(d) Avoid executing during high gas periods for non-urgent transactions

7.3 - Smart Contract Interactions

Before interacting with external smart contracts:
(a) Verify contract addresses through multiple sources
(b) Review contract audit status
(c) Understand approval mechanisms and revocation
(d) Set appropriate spending limits on approvals
(e) Revoke unused approvals quarterly

7.4 - Bridge Operations

Cross-chain transfers shall:
(a) Use official bridge contracts
(b) Verify bridge security and track record
(c) Test with small amounts first
(d) Document bridge transactions completely
(e) Account for bridge finalization times

                    SECTION 8: RISK MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

8.1 - Risk Categories

SMART CONTRACT RISK:
- Mitigation: Audited protocols, position limits, insurance
- Monitoring: Real-time alerts, security feeds

MARKET RISK:
- Mitigation: Diversification, stablecoin allocation
- Monitoring: Daily price tracking, rebalancing triggers

LIQUIDITY RISK:
- Mitigation: Operating reserves, diverse venues
- Monitoring: Liquidity depth analysis

COUNTERPARTY RISK:
- Mitigation: Due diligence, position limits
- Monitoring: Protocol health metrics

REGULATORY RISK:
- Mitigation: Legal counsel, compliance program
- Monitoring: Regulatory developments tracking

8.2 - Position Limits

| Asset Type          | Maximum % of Treasury |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Single token       | 30% (excluding TUT)  |
| Single protocol    | 20%                  |
| Single chain       | 60%                  |
| Illiquid assets    | 15%                  |
| Leverage           | 0% (without approval)|

8.3 - Insurance

The DAO shall maintain:
(a) Smart contract coverage (Nexus Mutual, etc.)
(b) Crime/theft insurance where available
(c) D&O coverage for signers and contributors
(d) Cyber liability insurance

================================================================================
                  SECTION 10: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
================================================================================

10.1 - Emergency Definition

Treasury emergencies include:
(a) Smart contract exploits or hacks
(b) Protocol failures affecting positions
(c) Rapid market deterioration
(d) Regulatory enforcement actions
(e) Loss of access to treasury assets

10.2 - Emergency Response

IMMEDIATE (0-4 hours):
- Assess nature and scope of emergency
- Activate emergency communication channels
- Pause affected protocols if possible
- Notify all multi-sig signers

SHORT-TERM (4-48 hours):
- Execute emergency asset protection
- Document all actions taken
- Communicate status to Members
- Engage legal/security experts as needed

RECOVERY (48+ hours):
- Full incident analysis
- Develop recovery plan
- Governance review of response
- Policy updates based on lessons learned

10.3 - Emergency Powers

In genuine emergencies, the multi-sig may:
(a) Move assets to safety without governance vote
(b) Pause protocol interactions
(c) Revoke smart contract approvals
(d) Engage emergency services/experts

All emergency actions require:
- Minimum 2-of-5 signers for asset protection
- Post-incident governance review within 7 days
- Full documentation and transparency

In emergencies, temporary policy modifications may be enacted by:
(a) 4-of-5 multi-sig agreement
(b) Maximum duration: 30 days
(c) Governance ratification required within 14 days
(d) Full disclosure to Members
