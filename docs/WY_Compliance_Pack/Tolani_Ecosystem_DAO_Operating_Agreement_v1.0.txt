================================================================================
                    ARTICLE I - ORGANIZATION AND PURPOSE
================================================================================

Section 1.1 - Formation

Tolani Ecosystem DAO LLC (the "DAO" or "Company") is a Wyoming Decentralized 
Autonomous Organization Limited Liability Company formed pursuant to the 
Wyoming Limited Liability Company Act (W.S. 17-29-101 et seq.) and the 
Wyoming Decentralized Autonomous Organization Supplement (W.S. 17-31-101 
et seq.).

Section 1.2 - Name

The name of the Company is "Tolani Ecosystem DAO LLC" and may also be referred 
to as "Tolani DAO" or the "Tolani Ecosystem."

Section 1.3 - Principal Office

The principal office of the Company is located in the State of Wyoming. The 
registered agent and office are as designated in the Articles of Organization 
filed with the Wyoming Secretary of State.

Section 1.4 - Purpose

The Company is organized for the following purposes:

(a) To operate a decentralized educational technology platform providing 
    workforce development, technical training, and skills certification 
    programs aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals;

(b) To issue, manage, and govern the TUT (Tolani Utility Token) governance 
    token and associated utility tokens (uTUT) for training rewards;

(c) To facilitate decentralized finance (DeFi) operations including token 
    staking, liquidity provision, and yield optimization for ecosystem 
    sustainability;

(d) To fund and support educational initiatives, particularly in underserved 
    communities, through decentralized treasury management;

(e) To engage in any lawful activity for which a Wyoming DAO LLC may be 
    organized.

Section 1.5 - Duration

The Company shall have perpetual existence unless dissolved pursuant to 
Article XI of this Agreement or applicable law.

                         ARTICLE II - DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

Section 2.1 - Defined Terms

"Blockchain" means the Ethereum and Base network public distributed ledgers 
where the DAO's smart contracts and tokens are deployed.

"Gnosis Safe" means the multi-signature smart contract wallet at address 
0xa56eb5E3990C740C8c58F02eAD263feF02567677 used for treasury management.

"Governor Contract" means the TolaniGovernor smart contract that manages 
on-chain voting and proposal execution.

"Member" means any holder of TUT governance tokens.

"Proposal" means a formal on-chain governance action submitted to the 
Governor Contract for voting.

"Quorum" means the minimum percentage of voting power required for a 
valid governance vote, set at 4% of total token supply.

"Smart Contract" means self-executing code deployed on the Blockchain 
that automatically enforces the rules defined in its programming.

"TUT Token" means the ERC-20 governance token of the DAO with contract 
address 0x90e9d7189D605a824C2481Fe88A1d9A7DDFAF71D on Ethereum Mainnet 
and 0xAf7e938741a720508897Bf3a13538f6713A337A4 on Base.

"uTUT Token" means the non-transferable utility token awarded to Members 
who complete training programs.

"Timelock" means the TolaniTimelock smart contract that enforces a delay 
period between proposal approval and execution.

"Voting Period" means the duration during which Members may cast votes 
on a Proposal, currently set at 7 days (approximately 50,400 blocks).

                 ARTICLE III - MEMBERSHIP AND TUT TOKENS
================================================================================

Section 3.1 - Membership Qualification

Membership in the DAO is acquired by obtaining TUT governance tokens through 
any lawful means, including:

(a) Participation in token distribution events;
(b) Purchase on secondary markets;
(c) Receipt through governance-approved allocations;
(d) Earning through ecosystem participation and staking.

Section 3.2 - Membership Rights

Each Member holding TUT tokens shall have the right to:

(a) Participate in governance voting in proportion to their token holdings;
(b) Submit Proposals to the Governor Contract (subject to proposal threshold);
(c) Delegate voting power to another address;
(d) Receive distributions approved by governance;
(e) Access DAO-funded educational resources and training programs.

Section 3.3 - Membership Responsibilities

Each Member agrees to:

(a) Exercise voting rights in good faith for the benefit of the DAO;
(b) Comply with applicable laws and regulations;
(c) Not engage in activities that harm the DAO or its reputation;
(d) Respect the outcomes of legitimate governance votes.

Section 3.4 - Token Supply and Distribution

The TUT token has a maximum supply of 100,000,000 (one hundred million) tokens 
with 18 decimal places. The initial allocation is as follows:

| Allocation Category        | Percentage | Amount (TUT)    |
|---------------------------|------------|-----------------|
| DAO Treasury              | 40%        | 40,000,000      |
| Team & Advisors (Vested)  | 20%        | 20,000,000      |
| Ecosystem Development     | 15%        | 15,000,000      |
| Community Rewards         | 15%        | 15,000,000      |
| Liquidity Provision       | 10%        | 10,000,000      |

Section 3.5 - No Personal Liability

No Member shall be personally liable for any debt, obligation, or liability 
of the DAO solely by reason of being a Member.

                   ARTICLE IV - GOVERNANCE AND VOTING
================================================================================

Section 4.1 - Governance Framework

The DAO operates under a hybrid governance model combining:

(a) On-chain governance through the TolaniGovernor smart contract;
(b) Off-chain discussion and signaling through community forums;
(c) Multi-signature treasury controls through Gnosis Safe.

Section 4.2 - Voting Power

Voting power is determined by:

(a) The number of TUT tokens held by a Member at the time of proposal creation;
(b) Tokens must be self-delegated or delegated to vote;
(c) One (1) TUT token equals one (1) vote.

Section 4.3 - Proposal Process

Proposals follow this process:

1. DISCUSSION PHASE: Community discussion on governance forums (recommended 
   minimum 3 days);

2. PROPOSAL SUBMISSION: Any Member meeting the proposal threshold may submit 
   a Proposal to the Governor Contract;

3. VOTING DELAY: A 1-day delay between proposal submission and voting start;

4. VOTING PERIOD: Members vote For, Against, or Abstain during the 7-day 
   voting period;

5. TIMELOCK: Approved proposals enter a 48-hour timelock before execution;

6. EXECUTION: Proposals are executed automatically or by any Member calling 
   the execute function.

Section 4.4 - Voting Thresholds

| Parameter              | Value                        |
|-----------------------|------------------------------|
| Proposal Threshold    | 100,000 TUT (0.1% of supply) |
| Quorum                | 4% of total voting power     |
| Approval Threshold    | Simple majority (>50%)       |
| Voting Delay          | 1 day (~7,200 blocks)        |
| Voting Period         | 7 days (~50,400 blocks)      |
| Timelock Delay        | 48 hours                     |

Section 4.5 - Vote Types

Members may cast votes as follows:

(a) FOR - Support the proposal;
(b) AGAINST - Oppose the proposal;
(c) ABSTAIN - Counted for quorum but not for/against.

Section 4.6 - Delegation

Members may delegate their voting power to any Ethereum address. Delegation 
does not transfer token ownership and may be revoked at any time.

Section 5.1 - Management Structure

Pursuant to W.S. 17-31-104(e), this DAO is algorithmically managed, meaning:

(a) The underlying smart contracts are capable of updating, modifying, or 
    otherwise upgrading themselves through governance votes;

(b) Day-to-day operations are executed by smart contracts without human 
    intervention;

(c) Material changes require governance approval through the Governor Contract.

Section 5.3 - Administrative Safeguards

The following multi-signature safeguards apply:

(a) Treasury disbursements over $10,000 USD equivalent require 3-of-5 
    Gnosis Safe signatures;

(b) Smart contract upgrades require governance approval AND multi-sig 
    execution;

(c) Emergency pause functionality requires 2-of-5 signatures.

Section 5.4 - Registered Agent

The DAO maintains a registered agent in Wyoming as required by W.S. 17-31-106. 
The registered agent may:

(a) Receive service of process on behalf of the DAO;
(b) Provide notice to Members as required by law;
(c) Execute ministerial duties as directed by governance.

================================================================================
              ARTICLE VI - CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS
================================================================================

Section 6.1 - Capital Contributions

Members are not required to make capital contributions beyond their initial 
token acquisition. Additional capital may be raised through:

(a) Token sales approved by governance;
(b) Protocol revenue (transaction fees, staking rewards);
(c) Grants and donations;
(d) DeFi yield generation.

Section 6.2 - Distributions

Distributions to Members, if any, shall be:

(a) Approved by governance vote;
(b) Distributed pro-rata based on TUT token holdings;
(c) Subject to treasury sustainability requirements;
(d) Compliant with applicable tax and securities laws.

Section 6.3 - Profit and Loss Allocation

The DAO operates primarily for ecosystem development and educational purposes. 
Any net income shall be:

(a) First, allocated to treasury reserves;
(b) Second, allocated to ecosystem development;
(c) Third, distributed to Members as approved by governance.

                    ARTICLE VII - TREASURY MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

Section 7.1 - Treasury Composition

The DAO Treasury consists of:

(a) On-chain assets held in smart contracts and the Gnosis Safe;
(b) Fiat currency held in traditional banking accounts;
(c) Digital assets across multiple blockchain networks.

Section 7.2 - Treasury Controls

Treasury operations are subject to the DAO Treasury Policy document, including:

(a) Spending approval thresholds;
(b) Multi-signature requirements;
(c) Investment guidelines;
(d) Audit requirements.

Section 7.3 - Authorized Expenditures

The Treasury may fund:

(a) Educational program development and delivery;
(b) Technology infrastructure and development;
(c) Marketing and community growth;
(d) Legal and compliance costs;
(e) Operational expenses;
(f) Grant programs for ecosystem projects.

================================================================================
                  ARTICLE VIII - SMART CONTRACT GOVERNANCE
================================================================================

Section 8.1 - Smart Contract Authority

Pursuant to W.S. 17-31-105, the smart contracts listed in Section 5.2 shall 
have the authority to:

(a) Execute approved governance proposals;
(b) Manage token distributions and transfers;
(c) Enforce time-locked operations;
(d) Facilitate voting and delegation.

Section 8.2 - Smart Contract Amendments

Any amendment to core smart contracts requires:

(a) A governance proposal with detailed specification;
(b) Independent security audit for material changes;
(c) Approval by governance vote meeting quorum;
(d) Execution through the Timelock contract.

Section 8.3 - Conflict Resolution

In the event of conflict between this Operating Agreement and smart contract 
code:

(a) For operational matters, smart contract code shall control;
(b) For legal interpretation, this Operating Agreement shall control;
(c) Governance may vote to reconcile conflicts through amendments.

Section 8.4 - Public Identifier

Pursuant to W.S. 17-31-106(b), the DAO's publicly available identifier is 
the TUT token contract address on Ethereum Mainnet:
0x90e9d7189D605a824C2481Fe88A1d9A7DDFAF71D

                  ARTICLE IX - LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION
================================================================================

Section 9.1 - Limited Liability

To the fullest extent permitted by Wyoming law:

(a) No Member shall be liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities 
    of the DAO;

(b) No Member shall be liable for the actions of other Members;

(c) The DAO's liability shall be limited to its assets.

Section 9.2 - Indemnification

The DAO shall indemnify any Member, manager, or agent against claims arising 
from good faith actions taken on behalf of the DAO, to the extent permitted 
by law and approved by governance.

Section 9.3 - Insurance

The DAO may maintain insurance coverage as approved by governance, including:

(a) Smart contract security insurance;
(b) Directors and officers liability insurance;
(c) Cyber liability insurance.

================================================================================
                         ARTICLE X - AMENDMENTS
================================================================================

Section 10.1 - Amendment Process

This Operating Agreement may be amended by:

(a) A governance proposal submitted to the Governor Contract;
(b) Approval by majority vote meeting quorum requirements;
(c) Execution through the standard timelock process;
(d) Publication of amended agreement.

Section 10.2 - Notice

All amendments shall be:

(a) Published on the DAO's official website and documentation;
(b) Announced through official communication channels;
(c) Recorded with a blockchain transaction hash for verifiability.

================================================================================
                        ARTICLE XI - DISSOLUTION
================================================================================

Section 11.1 - Voluntary Dissolution

The DAO may be dissolved by:

(a) A governance proposal for dissolution;
(b) Approval by supermajority (66%) of voting power;
(c) Compliance with W.S. 17-31-114 and 17-29-802;
(d) Distribution of remaining assets to Members pro-rata.

Section 11.2 - Winding Up

Upon dissolution:

(a) All debts and obligations shall be satisfied;
(b) Remaining assets shall be distributed to Members;
(c) Smart contracts shall be retired or paused;
(d) Articles of Dissolution shall be filed with Wyoming Secretary of State.

================================================================================
                     ARTICLE XII - DISPUTE RESOLUTION
================================================================================

Section 12.1 - Internal Resolution

Disputes among Members shall first attempt resolution through:

(a) Direct communication between parties;
(b) Community mediation through governance forums;
(c) Binding arbitration if mediation fails.

Section 12.2 - Arbitration

Disputes not resolved internally shall be submitted to binding arbitration:

(a) Administered by the American Arbitration Association;
(b) Under the Commercial Arbitration Rules;
(c) Conducted in the State of Wyoming;
(d) With judgment entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

Section 12.3 - Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Wyoming, 
specifically the Wyoming LLC Act (W.S. 17-29-101 et seq.) and the 
Wyoming DAO Supplement (W.S. 17-31-101 et seq.).
